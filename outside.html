Outside Work

When I started college at University of North Carolina at Greensboro in the fall of 2011, I had envisioned myself to do something great. I wanted to make an impact wherever I went. I wanted to be a part of a community. I wanted to see my imagination come to life. I choose to be a web developer to bring my ideas to life and help other see what I think a better world looks like.

The world wide web is everywhere, and majority of our population is on the world wide web. In today’s era, information technology and the advancement of web developers and designers are becoming more of a necessity. I want to contribute to that. I want to be part of an astonishing and innovative dream within this rising technological world. That is why I set on becoming a web developer and informational system specialist.

About the past 3+ years I have worked in a sales environment at Starbucks and have spent the last 2+ years with the company in a management role. In each of these positions, I have gained valuable experience and utilized my organizational skills and personable attitude to ensure goals are consistently met and guaranteeing complete customer satisfaction.

In addition, to my experience at Starbucks, I have also attended multiple tours learning about different companies such as Replacements Ltd., Transplace, Lewis Storage, and EPES Transport understanding their use of databases as well as different types of logistics and transportation systems.

With all this, I am a customer service-oriented professional with excellent communication and relationship building skills. I have also self-taught and became proficient in Excel (VBA), SQL, and PHP with the drive to learn more. This is who I am. My experiences as front end developer, database inventory, and in sales have taught me to be a prepared and effective IT specialist.
